Pre-registration

Preregistration Preregistration is the practice of documenting your research plan at the beginning of your study and storing that plan in a read-only public repository, such as OSF Registries or As Predicted.org. Benefits of Preregistration Clarity: Ensures that research teams share a clear understanding of their research goals and processes. Transparency: Separates the confirmatory aspects of the research from the exploratory aspects. Collaboration: Allows for input from colleagues online prior to conducting the study. Pre-acceptance: Enables journal editors to accept researchersâ€™ papers conditionally, regardless of the outcomes. Opensesame

OpenSesame is a program to create experiments for psychology, neuroscience, and experimental economics. It does not require coding skills to create psycholinguistics experiments and is particularly friendly to beginners with a comprehensive graphical, point-and-click interface. You can find tutorials and example experiments on the website that are highly useful in learning to build your own experiments. Opensesame experiments can be implemented both offline and online.
